Lise Zambelli

Céramiste

Créer avec la terre, donner vie à la matière, partager l'essence de la beauté naturelle

Découvrir mes œuvres

Je cherche à capturer l'essence même de la beauté naturelle, celle qui émane de l'imperfection parfaite, des formes organiques et des textures qui racontent une histoire. Mes créations ne sont pas de simples objets, mais des témoins d'un moment, d'une émotion, d'une réflexion sur notre rapport au monde.

Dans un monde de plus en plus dématérialisé, je ressens le besoin profond de créer des objets tangibles, porteurs de sens et d'émotion. La céramique me permet de matérialiser mes pensées, mes rêves et mes questionnements sur la beauté, l'éphémère et l'éternel.

« La céramique est une étreinte de la terre et du feu,
une trace de la nature qui prend forme entre nos mains. »

Lise Zambelli

Biography

Lise est née à Bordeaux, en France. Elle a déménagé en Italie en 2021, à Vinci First she was pianist and teaching piano in conservatories. Then she studied decorative painting and story of Art in school of Louvre in Paris. After a ceramics residence in Tokoname, Japan, in 2010, she oriented her life to ceramics world. Many workshops, symposium in different countries: Korea, China, Finland, Taiwan, Hungary.... Exhibitions often shared, sometimes in solo as in December 2019 in Moscow, Russia.
For the second time, she is invited to go to Mauritius island in order to install a ceramics atelier for local people. Then through a gallery these ceramics are sold to tourists and hotels.
She like to do animals and bodies. But also she started to do monumental sculptures when collectors asked her to exhibit in a monumental ceramics exhibition. Of course The size of the kiln is a limit to build, that's why their sculptures are similar to totems composed with several pieces.
The most important is sharing ceramics knowledge with friends. She is member of ArtCeram2 in Sèvres since 2014 and member of International Association of Ceramics since 2019.
A symposium with monumental sculptures and sharing this experience with other ceramists would be a beautiful realization.
Languages spoken : French, Italian, English.
Languages studied at school : German and Russian
